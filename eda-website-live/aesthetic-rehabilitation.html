What you'll walk away with

Nine things you'll be able to do on Monday.

Not theory for theory's sake. Every module maps to a decision or a movement you'll make chairside.

01

Comprehensive rehabilitation diagnosis

A structured approach to complex cases: occlusion, vertical dimension, wear patterns, muscle involvement and functional risk.

02

Occlusal philosophies, demystified

Kois, Spear, Dawson, LVI and Pankey compared. How centric relation definitions differ, and when to apply each approach.

03

Centric relation & centric occlusion

Accurately identify, verify and record CR and CO, and understand how to transition CO=MIP.

04

Neuromuscular deprogramming

Hands-on proficiency with leaf gauges, Lucia jigs, Kois deprogrammers and anterior deprogrammers, and when to use each.

05

Vertical dimension management

When it is indicated to increase VDO and how to do so safely, predictably and accurately with structured protocols.

06

Orthotic therapy & stabilisation

Design, place and adjust orthotics, transition them into CO=MIP, and use them as diagnostic and therapeutic tools.

07

Advanced preparation strategies

Upper and lower arch preparation for rehab cases, including advanced lower arch, BOPT and vertical preparation protocols.

08

Equilibration & scheme design

Selectively equilibrate and establish stable, reproducible schemes, mutually protected and balanced bilateral occlusion.

09

Restorative sequencing & execution

Clear sequencing from diagnosis through to definitive restorative delivery on complex cases.

Frequently asked

Everything you might be wondering.

Who is AAR for?
Experienced clinicians managing, or wanting to manage, complex restorative cases. If worn dentitions, collapsed bites and vertical dimension questions currently get referred out, this course is built to bring them back in-house with a defensible system.
How hands-on is it, really?
The core of the course. You'll identify and record CR and CO, work with leaf gauges, Lucia jigs, Kois and anterior deprogrammers, transition orthotics into CO=MIP, record vertical dimension changes with calipers, and practise advanced preparation and selective equilibration, all with individual faculty supervision.
What's the cohort size?
Kept small, with multiple educators on the floor. That ratio is the point, you get direct feedback from clinicians who place these cases every week.
Is there CPD credit?
Yes. Attendance provides 16 hours of CPD, with a certificate of attendance detailing hours and learning outcomes.
How do I pay, and what's the refund policy?
Booking and payment go through Eventbrite. Cancellation policy: more than 30 days before the course date, a full refund. Within 30 days, a 50% refund. Within 15 days, no refund is available. This reflects the limited number of places and the resources required to deliver a high quality hands on experience.
Is there support after the course?
Yes. Every graduate gets ongoing faculty support for cases you're planning. The course is the start, not the end.
