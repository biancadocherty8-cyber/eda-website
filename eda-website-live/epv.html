Quick answers.

This is not a lecture course. Both days are structured around you doing the dentistry. Here is the full schedule.

Day One Diagnosis → Design → Preparation

9:00am · The Consultation Process. Structured new-patient assessment, comprehensive EO/IO exam, records (photography, digital scan, impressions, radiographs). Ethical case selection and conversion.

10:00am · Smile Design. Universally accepted principles. Facial, dental and gingival parameters. Translating 2D into a predictable 3D result, and communicating it to patient and lab.

11:00am · Motivational Mock-Up. The role of mock-ups in diagnostic validation, visual communication and expectation management.

Afternoon · Material Biochemistry & Preparation Design, hands-on. Surface treatment protocols, bonding science, and a full overview of Type I to IV veneer preparations. You prep 12 teeth on models under direct supervision.

Late PM · Soft Tissue Management. Surgical and non-surgical crown lengthening. Black-triangle management.

Day Two Impressions → Temporise → Bond

Morning · Impression Techniques & Occlusion. Analogue vs digital workflows. Occlusal philosophies and how they influence veneer longevity.

Late AM · Temporisation, hands-on. Three predictable methods. Soft tissue stability, durability, and ease of removal.

Afternoon · Cementation Protocol, hands-on. Isolation, retraction, resin cement selection, bonding. When to bond versus lute.

Late PM · Case Studies & Q&A. Complex case reviews, complication management, and open Q&A with the faculty.

If you're comfortable with crown and bridge, you're ready. You do not need prior veneer experience.

The system takes you from first veneer consult to confident delivery, and the educator-to-attendee ratio means faculty work with you at whatever level you arrive. Cohorts typically include dentists placing their first veneers alongside dentists refining an existing workflow, and the format works for both.

Genuinely hands-on. Over the two days you complete four supervised exercises with an educator at your bench giving live feedback:

1 · Prescribe smile designs across five cases. Work through five real patient cases, diagnosing, planning and prescribing the design for each.

2 · Fabricate custom putty keys. Build incisal reduction, B-plane reduction and ideal mock-up keys from scratch.

3 · Minimal prep with the APT technique. Prepare 12 maxillary teeth covering Type I through Type IV porcelain veneer preparations, using the Aesthetic Pre-evaluative Temporary technique.

4 · Advanced temporisation. Produce ultra-cleansable temporaries focused on soft tissue stability, durability and ease of maintenance.

Cohorts are kept small with multiple educators on the floor, and all materials, models and burs are included.
