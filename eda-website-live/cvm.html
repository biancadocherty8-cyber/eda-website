Quick answers.

This is not a lecture course. Both days combine focused lectures with extensive hands-on training. Here is the full schedule, 9:00am to 5:00pm both days.

Day One Foundations, Design & Core Techniques

Welcome and Programme Orientation. Course objectives, composite philosophy and how direct composite veneers fit into contemporary aesthetic dentistry.

Resin Veneer Indications and Case Selection. A structured approach to identifying suitable cases, managing aesthetic risk, understanding limitations and setting realistic patient expectations.

Smile Design for Composite. Proportion, symmetry and incisal edge design applied specifically to direct composite cases.

Material Selection and Handling, hands-on. Contemporary composite systems, shade selection and handling for aesthetics, efficiency and longevity.

Monochromatic and Micro Layering Techniques, hands-on. Efficient monochromatic veneers, then micro facial layering for depth, translucency and surface character.

Day Two Advanced Layering, Finishing & Clinical Execution

Day 2 Orientation and Review. Recap of Day 1 concepts and objectives for advanced aesthetic execution.

Advanced Layering for High Aesthetic Demand Cases, hands-on. Full layering protocols for complex cases requiring maximal optical integration, depth and lifelike results.

Morphology and Surface Texture, hands-on. Refinement of tooth anatomy, line angles, embrasures and surface texture.

Finishing and Polishing Protocols, hands-on. Structured sequences for durable, high gloss surfaces that maintain aesthetics over time.

Minimally Invasive Composite Workflows. Conservative preparation and bonding strategies for enamel preservation, adhesion and predictability.

Clinical Efficiency and Predictability. Optimising workflows to balance artistry with time efficiency.

Case Review, Final Q&A and Course Close. Real world case presentations and discussion to consolidate learning and refine decision making.

If you place composite restorations day to day, you're ready. You do not need prior veneer experience.

The programme is sequential and systems based, so you understand each step of the workflow before you execute it. Cohorts typically include dentists placing their first composite veneers alongside dentists refining an existing layering technique, and the format works for both.

Hands-on sessions form the core of the course. You work through composite veneer techniques on models in a supervised, small group environment, with faculty giving individual feedback and refining your technique in real time:

1 · Monochromatic composite veneers. Simplified single-shade techniques for efficient, high quality delivery.

2 · Micro facial layering. Layering strategies for depth, translucency and surface character.

3 · Full layering protocols. Advanced multi-shade builds for high aesthetic demand cases.

4 · Morphology, finishing and polishing. Anatomy, line angles and texture, then structured finishing sequences for a durable high gloss.

Cohorts are kept small with multiple educators on the floor, and all materials, models and consumables are included.
